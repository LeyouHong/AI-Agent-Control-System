# Web Search RAG Rules

## Purpose
This document outlines the guidelines and best practices for conducting web searches to gather information efficiently and effectively.

### 1. Define Clear Objectives
- Identify the specific information needed before conducting a search.
- Frame questions clearly to guide the search process.

### 2. Use Specific Keywords
- Utilize targeted keywords relevant to the query.
- Include variations of phrases and terms for broader results.

### 3. Evaluate Sources
- Prioritize reputable and authoritative sources (e.g., educational institutions, government sites, major news outlets).
- Cross-reference information from multiple sources to ensure accuracy.

### 4. Summarize Findings
- After gathering information, summarize key points and relevant data succinctly.
- Highlight sources for each piece of information to maintain credibility.

### 5. Stay Updated
- Regularly check for the latest information, as facts can change over time.
- Use alerts or subscriptions to stay informed about specific topics.

### 6. Ethical Considerations
- Respect copyright and intellectual property rights when using information.
- Properly attribute or link back to original sources when sharing findings.

### Conclusion
Following these rules will enhance the effectiveness of web searches and improve the quality of information gathered.
